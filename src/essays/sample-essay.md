This is demonstration content with a single purpose: to show what the essay page looks like once every piece is assembled — the heading, the hero illustration, the text column, a pulled quote, and the subscription section. It is not Artur's voice or a preview of the blog's content, only scaffolding used to check the mockup in working code.

The eventual blog will be a collection of essays about working at the intersection of artificial intelligence, film and robotics — written while the studio is being built, not after the fact. This sample entry was used to verify typography (Fraunces for headings, Newsreader for body text), the 660px column width, and behaviour across both colour themes.

> This is a sample pulled quote — it checks the `.pull` block style, the Fraunces italic, and the centred layout in both palettes.

Below the quote, a regular paragraph resumes, to show the transition back to body text and the spacing between blocks. In a real essay, this is where the argument would continue, built from concrete examples from the studio's work.

The final paragraph closes the demo — further down the page, the author's monogram, the living element (the bird), and the newsletter box follow, all rendered independently of this text.
